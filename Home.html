Preamble for Investigative Procedures

This is an investigation in the Department of Sociology and Criminology and the Program of Atmospheric Sciences at Howard University. Dr. Terri Adams, an Associate Professor at Howard University, is conducting the study. This study is designed to assess your reactions to the presentation of textual and numerical weather forecasts. There are no right or wrong answers to the questions you will be asked. Our sole interest is in your thoughts, opinions, and reactions.

The benefit to you participating in this study is that you will be able to articulate how differences in presented weather information impact your decision-making during weather emergencies. We anticipate minimal psychological risks and personal time inconvenience. Unfortunately, budgetary constraints will not allow for financial compensation.

The results of this research will be used for the development of decision-support tools to assist weather forecasters and emergency managers. You will remain anonymous throughout the course of this study. The procedures for maintaining confidentiality are as follows: (1) individual results will be pooled with group results; (2) you will not be asked to include any personally identifying information on the questionnaires (e.g. name, telephone number, address, or specific birth information).

Participation in the study is completely voluntary and you may withdraw from this study at any time. The participants should be 18 years of age or older and in good health. If you are younger than 18, please contact the investigator immediately.

If you would like any further information about this study, please contact Dr. Terri Adams at 202-806-6700. You may also contact the Howard University Institutional Review Board at 202-865-8597 from 8:30 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday, or email theorrc@howard.edu, if you would like to discuss this study with someone other than the investigators.
